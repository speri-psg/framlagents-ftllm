FINTRAC Guidance: Reporting Suspicious Transactions (STRs)
Source: https://fintrac-canafe.canada.ca/guidance-directives/transaction-operation/str-dod/str-dod-eng
Effective: June 1, 2021 (updated 2024)

CORE REQUIREMENT
All reporting entities must submit Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs) to FINTRAC "as soon as practicable" after establishing reasonable grounds to suspect money laundering or terrorist financing. No monetary threshold applies.

KEY DEFINITIONS

Reasonable Grounds to Suspect: A threshold above "gut feeling" but below "reasonable grounds to believe." Requires considering facts, context, and relevant indicators to conclude a possibility exists that an offense occurred — without needing verification or proof.

Facts: Events, actions, or occurrences that exist or are known to have happened (not opinions).

Context: Information clarifying circumstances or explaining situations, derived from business awareness, client knowledge, and risk assessments.

As Soon as Practicable: After completing measures establishing the suspicion threshold, submission must be treated as a priority. Delays require justification.

WHO MUST REPORT
- All reporting entities and their employees
- Employees only if employers fail to report (rare)
- Service providers may submit on behalf of entities, but legal responsibility cannot be delegated

WHAT TRIGGERS REPORTING
Report when suspicion relates to:
- Money laundering offenses (including attempts)
- Terrorist activity financing offenses (including attempts)
- Sanctions evasion (when applicable)

CONFIDENTIALITY PROTECTION
"No person or entity will be prosecuted for sending a Suspicious Transaction Report in good faith."
You cannot inform clients of report contents or that reporting occurred if this prejudices investigations.

SUBMISSION METHODS
Electronic (mandatory if technically capable):
- FINTRAC Web Reporting System (FWR)
- FINTRAC API (available April 8, 2024 onward)

Paper (only if lacking technical capability):
- Fax: 1-866-226-2346
- Mail: FINTRAC, 234 Laurier Avenue West, 24th floor, Ottawa, ON K1P 1H7

REPORT STRUCTURE (six sections)
1. General Information: Business details, reference numbers
2. Transaction Information: Status, date, time, method, location, purpose
3. Starting Action: Direction (in/out), fund types, amounts, sources, conductor details
4. Completing Action: Disposition, amounts, beneficiary information
5. Details of Suspicion: Narrative explaining facts, context, and indicators supporting suspicion
6. Action Taken: Description of responses taken

Each report can contain multiple transactions with multiple starting/completing actions.

MANDATORY CONTENT STANDARDS
Reports must include:
- All parties' identifying information (conductors, third parties, beneficiaries, account holders)
- Transaction specifics (dates, times, amounts, methods)
- For entities: ownership, control, director, and beneficial owner information
- Clear explanation linking facts and indicators to the suspicion threshold
- Plain language — no organizational jargon or internal acronyms

What to Include:
- All email addresses, phone numbers, aliases, and identifiers
- Account numbers, virtual currency addresses, and reference numbers
- Relationships between parties
- Source of funds information
- Beneficial ownership details

What to Avoid:
- Using "reasonable grounds to believe" (higher standard than required)
- Omitting available party information from structured fields
- Summarizing transactions only in narrative sections
- Vague suspicion explanations lacking fact-to-indicator connections

TIMING AND CHANGES
Submit within reasonable timeframe after assessment completion.
To modify a submitted report: provide explanation and resubmit within 20 days of change request.

ADDITIONAL SIMULTANEOUS OBLIGATIONS
When suspicious transactions involve reportable amounts, also submit where applicable:
- Large Cash Transaction Reports (LCTR)
- Electronic Funds Transfer Reports (EFT)
- Large Virtual Currency Transaction Reports (LVCTR)
- Casino Disbursement Reports
- Listed Person/Entity Property Reports

RECORD RETENTION
Keep copies of submitted STRs for minimum 5 years from submission date.

QUALITY EXPECTATIONS
FINTRAC evaluates every STR received. Reports related to national security threats receive expedited 24-hour analysis.
Quality assessments address:
- Consistency in detection and submission practices
- Timeliness of reporting post-assessment
- Information completeness and accuracy
- KYC data integrity

SUBSEQUENT REPORTING
Continue submitting STRs for the same person/entity as long as suspicion persists.
Reference prior reports by including reporting entity reference numbers and transaction reference numbers, noting new facts or indicators identified.

CANADA vs USA COMPARISON NOTE
Canada uses STRs (Suspicious Transaction Reports); the US equivalent is SARs (Suspicious Activity Reports).
Key difference: Canada has no dollar threshold for STR filing — any suspicious transaction regardless of amount must be reported.
US SARs have minimum thresholds ($5,000 for banks under BSA).
