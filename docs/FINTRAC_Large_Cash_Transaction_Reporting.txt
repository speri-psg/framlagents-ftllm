FINTRAC Guidance: Reporting Large Cash Transactions (LCTRs)
Source: https://fintrac-canafe.canada.ca/guidance-directives/transaction-operation/lctr-doie/lctr-doie-eng
Effective: June 1, 2021

REPORTING THRESHOLD
A large cash transaction occurs when a reporting entity receives $10,000 CAD or more in cash in a single transaction.
"Cash" includes: coins and bank notes (Canadian and foreign), and fiat currency.
"Cash" excludes: cheques, money orders, and virtual currency.

SUBMISSION TIMELINE
Reports must be submitted to FINTRAC within 15 calendar days after receiving the cash.

THE 24-HOUR AGGREGATION RULE
Entities must report when they receive two or more cash amounts totaling $10,000+ within a consecutive 24-hour window if they know the transactions are:
- Conducted by the same person or entity
- Conducted on behalf of the same third party, or
- For the same beneficiary

FOREIGN CURRENCY CONVERSION
Use exchange rates published by the Bank of Canada at transaction time.
If Bank of Canada rate unavailable, apply the rate established in normal business operations, documented in compliance policies.

SUBMISSION METHODS
Reports must be submitted electronically via:
- FINTRAC Web Reporting System (FWR)
- FINTRAC API report submission
Paper forms permitted only when electronic submission is technically infeasible.

KEY EXEMPTIONS
No reporting required for cash received from:
- Financial entities
- Public bodies
- Persons acting on behalf of such entities

Life insurance companies have additional exemptions for:
- Reinsurance
- Registered annuities
- Reverse mortgages

REQUIRED INFORMATION CATEGORIES

General Information:
- Reporting entity number and location
- Aggregation type (conductor, third party, or beneficiary)
- 24-hour period dates/times with time zones

Transaction Details:
- Date, time, and method
- Purpose and threshold indicator
- Location where transaction occurred

Starting Action:
- Cash amount and currency
- Source of cash information
- Conductor details (person or entity)
- Third party information if applicable

Completing Action:
- Disposition type (deposit, exchange, transfer, investment, etc.)
- Amount and currency
- Account information
- Beneficiary details
- Other involved parties

FIELD DESIGNATIONS
- Mandatory (marked with *)
- Mandatory for processing (marked with ‡)
- Mandatory if applicable (marked with †)
- Reasonable measures (non-mandatory fields requiring good-faith effort)

IDENTITY VERIFICATION REQUIREMENTS
Entities must verify identity of the person or entity conducting the transaction using FINTRAC-prescribed documentation methods (see separate identity verification guidance).

RECORD RETENTION
Copies of submitted reports must be retained for at least 5 years from creation date.

RELATED OBLIGATIONS
Entities may need to simultaneously file:
- Suspicious Transaction Reports if money laundering indicators exist
- Electronic Funds Transfer Reports if dispositions involve reportable transfers

COMPLIANCE PROGRAM REQUIREMENTS
Policies must document processes for:
- Exchange rate selection and sources
- Transaction aggregation methodology
- Entity vs. person determination
- Report submission procedures
- Staff training on AML/CFT activities

CANADA vs USA COMPARISON NOTE
Canada: LCTR threshold = $10,000 CAD, filed within 15 calendar days.
USA: CTR (Currency Transaction Report) threshold = $10,000 USD, filed within 15 calendar days.
Key differences:
- Canada has no exemption program equivalent to the US CTR Exemptions (Phase I/Phase II)
- Every transaction over $10K CAD must be reported regardless of customer type
- US allows banks to exempt certain "non-listed" business customers from CTR filing
- Canada's 24-hour aggregation rule mirrors the US "structuring" aggregation concept
