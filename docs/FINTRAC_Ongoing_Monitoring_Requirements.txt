FINTRAC Guidance: Ongoing Monitoring Requirements
Source: https://fintrac-canafe.canada.ca/guidance-directives/client-clientele/omr-eng
Effective: June 1, 2021

OVERVIEW
This guidance applies to all reporting entities under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (PCMLTFA). Ongoing monitoring is a systematic review process that all entities with business relationships must conduct.

KEY DEFINITIONS

Ongoing Monitoring: A systematic review process examining client information to:
- Identify suspicious transactions requiring FINTRAC reporting
- Keep identification and beneficial ownership data current
- Reassess client risk levels
- Verify transaction consistency with client profiles

Business Relationship: A relationship that continues beyond a single transaction; typically formed when an account is opened or ongoing services are provided.

CORE REQUIREMENTS

When Monitoring Applies:
Reporting entities must "periodically conduct" ongoing monitoring for all business relationships. Frequency is based on risk assessment:
- Low-risk clients: less frequent reviews
- High-risk clients: enhanced measures required

ENHANCED ONGOING MONITORING
High-risk clients require additional protective measures including:
- Management-approved transaction review schedules
- More frequent high-risk transaction reports
- Flagging unusual activities
- Setting account limits triggering mandatory reviews
- Frequent transaction screening against suspicious indicators

EXCEPTIONS
Two categories are exempt from ongoing monitoring requirements:
1. Financial entities: Group plan accounts in dividend/distribution reinvestment plans (if sponsor operates in FATF member countries)
2. Insurance businesses: Reinsurance transactions

RECORD-KEEPING REQUIREMENTS
Documentation must include:
- Monitoring processes and procedures
- Enhanced monitoring protocols for high-risk clients
- Information obtained from monitoring activities
- Records on client identification updates

Retention: Minimum five years from creation date.

MONITORING CESSATION
- Standard monitoring ends when business relationships terminate
- Enhanced monitoring ends either at relationship termination or when clients no longer pose high risk (regular monitoring continues at appropriate frequency)

RISK-BASED APPROACH
The frequency and depth of monitoring must be calibrated to the entity's risk-based assessment of each client. Factors influencing risk level include:
- Customer type (individual, business, politically exposed person)
- Geographic risk (high-risk jurisdictions, FATF-identified countries)
- Product/service risk (cash-intensive products, international transfers)
- Transaction patterns and volumes
- Source of funds complexity

POLITICALLY EXPOSED PERSONS (PEPs)
Foreign PEPs: Automatically high-risk; enhanced ongoing monitoring is mandatory.
Domestic PEPs and heads of international organizations: High-risk if assessed as such; determine within 30 days of establishing a business relationship or of becoming aware of PEP status.

BENEFICIAL OWNERSHIP
Entities must take reasonable measures to obtain beneficial ownership information for entities with which they have business relationships. This information must be kept current as part of ongoing monitoring.

Beneficial owner: Any individual who directly or indirectly owns or controls 25% or more of an entity, or controls the entity by other means.

CANADA vs USA COMPARISON NOTE
Canada (FINTRAC/PCMLTFA): Requires "periodic" ongoing monitoring for all business relationships; frequency determined by risk.
USA (FinCEN CDD Rule, 31 CFR 1020.210): Requires "ongoing monitoring for the purpose of identifying and reporting suspicious transactions" and "updating customer information on a risk basis."

Key differences:
- Canada explicitly requires monitoring cessation procedures
- US CDD Rule (2016) added a 5th pillar (beneficial ownership) to existing AML program requirements
- Both require enhanced due diligence for high-risk customers
- Canada's 25% beneficial ownership threshold matches the US FinCEN CDD Rule threshold
