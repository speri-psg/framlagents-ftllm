FINTRAC Guidance: Record Keeping Requirements for Financial Entities
Source: https://fintrac-canafe.canada.ca/guidance-directives/recordkeeping-document/record/fin-eng
Effective: June 1, 2021

OVERVIEW
Financial entities must maintain records under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act (PCMLTFA) and associated Regulations.

CORE RECORD CATEGORIES AND RETENTION PERIODS

Reports to FINTRAC (5-year retention from submission/creation):
- Suspicious Transaction Reports (STRs): Keep copies; retain 5 years from submission
- Listed Person/Entity Property Reports: Keep copies; retain 5 years from submission
- Large Cash Transaction Reports (LCTRs): Keep copies; retain 5 years from creation
- Large Virtual Currency Transaction Reports: Keep copies; retain 5 years from creation
- Electronic Funds Transfer Reports: Keep copies; retain 5 years from creation

TRANSACTION RECORDS

Large Cash Transactions ($10,000+) — retain 5 years from creation:
- Date, account details, parties involved (name, address, DOB, occupation)
- Currency types/amounts
- Transaction purpose
- Receipt method
- Exchange rates
- Remittance details

Large Virtual Currency Transactions ($10,000+ equivalent) — retain 5 years:
- Date, account holder names, participant details
- VC types/amounts
- Exchange rates
- Transaction identifiers
- Blockchain addresses

$3,000+ Transactions — retain 5 years:
Required for traveller's cheques, money orders, and similar instruments.

Electronic Funds Transfers ($1,000+) — retain 5 years:
- Initiation details
- Beneficiary information
- Exchange rates
- Account numbers

Virtual Currency Transfers ($1,000+ equivalent) — retain 5 years:
- Transaction identifiers
- Blockchain addresses

EXCHANGE RECORDS (5-year retention)
- Foreign currency exchanges (all amounts)
- VC exchange transactions (all amounts)

ACCOUNT RECORDS (5 years from account closure)
- Account holder and authorized person details
- Signature cards
- Intended account use
- Applications
- Operating agreements
- Debit/credit memos
- Deposit slips
- Account statements
- Cleared cheque records
- Credit arrangement information

CREDIT CARD ACCOUNTS
- Account holder/authorized person records: 5 years from closure
- Applications: 5 years from closure
- Statements: 5 years from creation
- Transaction records: 5 years from creation

PREPAID PAYMENT PRODUCTS
- Account holder/authorized user records
- Applications: 5 years from closure
- Statements: 5 years from creation
- Payment slips
- Transaction records

TRUST RECORDS
Trust companies must keep:
- Trust deed copies
- Settlor information
- Corporate power-to-bind documentation (if applicable)
- Beneficiary records for inter vivos trusts

ACCESS AND STORAGE REQUIREMENTS
- Records must be provided to FINTRAC within 30 days of request
- Must be "readily accessible" via electronic or machine-readable formats
- Paper copies must be producible from stored formats

KEY EXEMPTIONS
- Credit card/PPP transactions when beneficiary has pre-existing merchant agreements
- Credit card acquiring activities
- Transactions involving other financial entities or public bodies
- Very large corporations and trusts (consolidated statements)
- Certain insurance and annuity products
- Registered plan accounts
- Virtual currency compensation for blockchain validation

DOCUMENTATION STANDARDS
When recording client information, entities should maximize descriptiveness — particularly regarding occupation or business nature — to establish transaction consistency with client profiles.

CANADA vs USA COMPARISON NOTE
Both Canada and the US require 5-year record retention for AML records.
Canada: PCMLTFA Regulations specify retention from creation or closure date.
USA: BSA (31 CFR 1010.430) requires records be retained for 5 years and be "retrievable within a reasonable period of time."
Key difference: Canada specifies "readily accessible" with a 30-day production window; US uses "reasonable period of time" (no fixed deadline).
