FINTRAC Guidance: Identity Verification — Methods and Triggers for Financial Entities
Sources:
- Methods: https://fintrac-canafe.canada.ca/guidance-directives/client-clientele/guide11/11-eng
- When to verify (Financial Entities): https://fintrac-canafe.canada.ca/guidance-directives/client-clientele/client/fin-eng
Effective: June 1, 2021

WHEN IDENTITY VERIFICATION IS REQUIRED (Financial Entities)

Transaction-Based Triggers:
1. Large cash transactions: $10,000 or more in cash
2. Large virtual currency transactions: $10,000 or more equivalent
3. Suspicious transactions: regardless of amount
4. Money orders/traveller's cheques: $3,000 or more
5. International EFTs: $1,000 or more when initiated
6. EFT beneficiaries: receiving $1,000 or more
7. Virtual currency transfers: $1,000 or more equivalent
8. VC to beneficiaries: $1,000 or more equivalent
9. Foreign currency exchange: $3,000 or more
10. VC exchange transactions: $1,000 or more equivalent

Account-Based Triggers:
- Account holders before first transaction (excluding initial deposit)
- Persons authorized to give account instructions
- Credit card account holders before activation
- Prepaid payment product (PPP) account holders and authorized users
- Trust settlors and co-trustees (within 15 days)

Payment-Based Triggers:
- Payments of $1,000 or more to PPP accounts

KEY EXEMPTIONS

Large Cash/VC Transactions:
- Received from another financial entity or public body
- Deposited to business accounts or automated banking machines

Suspicious Transactions:
- Already verified with no doubts remaining
- Verification would tip off the client

Account Openings:
- Existing clients opening subsequent accounts
- Persons already identified on business accounts (minimum 3 verified)
- Public bodies and very large corporations
- Mutual fund accounts (if dealer-verified)
- Life insurance beneficiary deposits (meeting specific criteria)

Other:
- Exempt insurance policies
- Registered plan accounts
- Certain escrow accounts
- Pension fund accounts
- Group plan members (if sponsor verified)

FIVE METHODS TO VERIFY PERSON IDENTITY

1. Government-Issued Photo ID Method
- Authentic, valid, and current document from federal, provincial, or territorial government
- Must include name, photo, and unique identifier
- Must match name and appearance of the person
- For remote verification: technology can assess authenticity (scanning, facial recognition)

2. Credit File Method
- Valid, current information from Canadian credit bureaus (Equifax, TransUnion)
- Credit file must exist for at least three years with multiple sources
- Search must occur at verification time — prior copies unacceptable
- Match name, address, and date of birth

3. Dual-Process Method
- Combines any two different reliable sources confirming: name and address, name and DOB, or name and financial account
- Information must be valid and current
- Cannot use same source twice
- Acceptable sources: utility bills, government statements, bank documents, property assessments

4. Affiliate or Member Method
- Relies on prior verification by affiliated reporting entities or credit union members using methods 1–3
- Verify that name, address, and DOB in affiliate records match information provided

5. Reliance Method
- Uses measures previously taken by another reporting entity or affiliated foreign entity
- Requires written agreement
- Must obtain information used for original verification
- Information must remain valid and current

THREE METHODS TO VERIFY ENTITY IDENTITY

1. Confirmation of Existence Method
- Corporations: Certificate of incorporation, annual securities filings, or active status documents
- Other Entities: Partnership agreements, articles of association, or existence confirmation records
- All documents must be authentic, valid, and current

2. Reliance Method
- Previous verification by another reporting entity or affiliated foreign entity
- Written agreement required

3. Simplified Identification Method
- Available only when risk assessment confirms low ML/TF risk
- Applies to: other reporting entities, foreign equivalents, regulated pension funds, publicly traded corporations, subsidiaries, foreign government institutions, public service bodies

ELECTRONIC VERIFICATION OPTIONS
For remote government ID verification, entities may:
- Use technology comparing document features against known characteristics
- Deploy facial recognition comparing applicant "selfie" to ID photo
- Conduct live video sessions comparing applicant to document
Process requirements must address authenticity determination and validity/currency confirmation.

RECORD KEEPING REQUIREMENTS
Photo ID: name, verification date, document type, number, jurisdiction, expiry
Credit File: name, search date, bureau/vendor name, file number
Dual-Process: name, verification date, two source names, information types, account/reference numbers
Entity Methods: registration numbers, document types, sources, or paper copies

DISTINCTION: VERIFICATION vs ONGOING MONITORING
Initial verification uses prescribed methods above.
Keeping client identification current is an ongoing monitoring obligation — does not require re-verification using prescribed methods.

CANADA vs USA COMPARISON NOTE
Canada (FINTRAC): Prescribes 5 specific methods for individual verification; 3 for entities.
USA (BSA/FinCEN CDD Rule): Requires "reasonable procedures" for verifying identity; prescribes a risk-based approach without specifying exact methods.

Key differences:
- Canada has a more prescriptive verification framework
- US Customer Identification Program (CIP) rules (31 CFR 1020.220) require name, DOB, address, and ID number — but method of verification is more flexible
- Both require beneficial ownership verification for entities (25% ownership threshold)
- Canada requires verification for EFTs at $1,000+ CAD; US requires CIP at account opening (not per-transaction)
